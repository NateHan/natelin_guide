## Eat

#### Restaurants

##### Hopper's
Soho + King's Cross · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Hopper%27s+London)

Sri Lankan food — absolutely delicious. Go to the Soho location right when it opens to put your name in, or you may wait hours. Not sure about the other location. Reserve online at hopperslondon.com.

##### Dishoom
Multiple locations · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Dishoom+London)

Slightly upscale Indian food with queues to match. Have a cocktail to pass the time — it's worth the wait.

##### Zaffrani
Islington · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Zaffrani+London)

Excellent Indian restaurant in my old neighborhood. Less known than Dishoom, equally good.

#### Casual & Everyday

##### Ottolenghi
Multiple locations · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Ottolenghi+London)

Israeli-inspired chef's local "chain." Can go just for pastries — some of the best I've ever had. Evening service is pricey; brunch is very good and cheaper.

##### V&A Museum Café
South Kensington · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=V%26A+Museum+Cafe+London)

The three adjoining dining rooms were designed by three different 19th-century designers (William Morris, Edward Poynter, and James Gamble) and are stunning. Eat parts of your meal in all three to get the full experience.

##### Fish and Chips
Citywide · $

Great in most places, but the best are the oldest, fussiest looking ones. Look for hole-in-the-wall "Chip Shop" joints — they'll probably serve it wrapped in newspaper. Pubs have it, but often upcharge for worse quality.

##### Grab-and-Go Sandwiches
Citywide · $

"Gross," one might think — but single-serve sandwiches from convenience and grocery stores are all fresh, cheap, and surprisingly tasty with wide variety. A perfect pick-me-up on a long day of sightseeing. My favorite is from a chain called **Eat**.

#### Worth the Detour

##### Gordon's Wine Bar
Embankment · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Gordon%27s+Wine+Bar+London)

A 19th-century warehouse turned candlelit wine bar — get wine and a cheese board (don't miss their smoked goat cheese, only served here). Perfect for an afternoon rest after sightseeing. On weekdays, arrive just before 5pm before it crowds up. Indoors is the moody underground experience; outdoors has a nice wine garden.

##### High Tea at The Orangerie
Kensington · $$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Orangerie+Kensington+London)

I have not been, but I have heard it's wonderful.

##### The Spaniard's Inn
Hampstead · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Spaniards+Inn+London)

A centuries-old pub worth checking out if you make it up to Hampstead Heath.

## Do

### Fortnum & Mason
Piccadilly · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Fortnum+%26+Mason+London)

Ultra-upscale grocery store and the Queen's official supplier of teas and biscuits. Their tea is exceptional if you're looking for loose-leaf English varieties. Good place for souvenirs.

### Primrose Hill / Regent's Park
[Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Primrose+Hill+London)

Nice open park. The top of the hill has a great view of the city.

### Regent's Canal
[Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Regent%27s+Canal+London)

A lovely riverwalk area connecting several neighbourhoods.

### Theater
Try a **TKTS** booth for last-minute tickets at a discount (around £30-40). The National Theatre also has good prices and excellent performances.

### Museums
All public museums in London have free admission. My favourite is probably the **V&A**, but I also highly recommend the **British Library** — their permanent collection ranges from a Gutenberg Bible to original Beatles lyrics in John Lennon's hand. The **National Portrait Gallery** is another favourite.

Smaller private ones worth checking out: **Leighton House Museum**, **John Soane's Museum**, and **Kenwood House** on Hampstead Heath. If you go to Kenwood, the garden adjoining their café is lovely for an afternoon tea on a nice day.

### Markets

**Borough Market** — Open most days, but Sat and Sun it's in full swing. Arrive hungry. Look for the cider stall and the mushroom pâté — SO good. [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Borough+Market+London)

**Brick Lane Market** — Street food, flea market shopping. Sat and Sun. [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Brick+Lane+Market+London)

**Portobello Road Market** — Street food, flea market. Saturdays only. [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Portobello+Road+Market+London)

## Day Trips

### Oxford
Easy to get to by train and completely different in feel from London. Go on a walking tour for access to a college or two. Highly recommend lunch or a drink at the **King's Arms** pub.

### Bath
A little further, but doable in a day. Make sure you go to the Roman Baths — if you're adventurous, you can "take the waters" like the Georgians.

### Windsor Castle
A quick afternoon trip — 20-30 min by train. Takes about 2-3 hours to explore. Great arms and armour collection.

### Hampton Court Palace
Main home of Henry VIII. Really neat.

### Kew Gardens
The Royal Botanical Gardens. On my list for next time.

## Tips

- Download **CityMapper** — better than Google Maps for London transit routes.
- Consider getting an **eSIM card** for data. 20 GB is around $15 USD and covers the whole continent.
- Download an **offline map** on Google Maps before you go, just in case.
- All public museums have free admission.
- Portobello Road is Saturdays only; Brick Lane is Sat and Sun.

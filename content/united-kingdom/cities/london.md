## Eat

### Gordon's Wine Bar
Embankment · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Gordon%27s+Wine+Bar+London)

A former 19th-century warehouse or ship-loading alcove turned into a candlelit wine bar. Go there for an afternoon aperitif — get some wine and then a few cheeses to snack on (don't miss their smoked goat cheese, only served there!). Good for a rest after an afternoon of sightseeing. If going on a weekday, get there just before 5, otherwise it crowds up. You can sit indoors or outdoors. Indoors is the cool "underground" experience, though the outdoors has a nice wine garden.

### Ottolenghi
Multiple locations · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Ottolenghi+London)

Israeli-inspired chef's local "chain" of restaurants. Can go there just for pastries, some of the best I've ever had. Their evening service is fairly pricey, but their brunch/breakfast is very good, too, and cheaper.

### V&A Museum Café
South Kensington · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=V%26A+Museum+Cafe+London)

The three adjoining dining rooms were designed by three different 19th-century designers (William Morris, Edward Poynter, and James Gamble), and are stunning. When Steph's mom and her went, we ate different parts of our meal in all three to get the full experience!

### Hopper's
Soho + King's Cross · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Hopper%27s+London)

Sri Lankan food — absolutely delicious. If you go to the one in Soho, go right when it opens to put your name in; if you don't, you may still have to wait for a few hours. Not sure about the other. https://www.hopperslondon.com/

### Dishoom
Multiple locations · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Dishoom+London)

Slightly upscale Indian food. May be quite a wait, but you can have a cocktail to pass the time.

### Zaffrani
Islington · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Zaffrani+London)

Excellent Indian restaurant in my old neighborhood.

### Fish and Chips
Citywide · $

It's great in most places, but the best places are the oldest, fussiest looking ones. They'll probably be called "Chip Shop" — look for hole-in-the-wall joints that serve it wrapped in newspaper for the best stuff. A lot of pubs have it, but will often upcharge for not-as-good quality.

### Grab-and-Go Sandwiches
Citywide · $

"Gross," one might say, as any "fresh" food from a 7-11 is anything but fresh. However, single-serve sandwiches are present in all convenience and grocery stores, and are all fresh, cheap, and tasty with a wide variety of types. They make a perfect easy pick-me-up on a long day of tourist-ing. My favorite is from a chain called **Eat**.

### High Tea at The Orangerie
Kensington · $$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Orangerie+Kensington+London)

I have not been, but I have heard it's wonderful.

### The Spaniard's Inn
Hampstead · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Spaniards+Inn+London)

A really neat centuries-old pub worth checking out if you go to Hampstead Heath.

## Do

### Fortnum & Mason
Piccadilly · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Fortnum+%26+Mason+London)

Basically an ultra-upscale grocery store — the Queen's official supplier of teas, snacks, etc. Their tea is really good if you're looking for loose-leaf English teas. Lots of snacks, cookies, etc. that are quintessentially British and all delicious. Good place for souvenirs and just fun to check out.

### Primrose Hill / Regent's Park
[Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Primrose+Hill+London)

Nice open park. The top of the hill has a great view of the city.

### Regent's Canal
[Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Regent%27s+Canal+London)

A nice Riverwalk area.

### Theater
If you're looking for theater tickets, try a **TKTS** (no typo) booth and you can get last-minute tickets on the cheap (maybe £30-40 per). The National Theatre also generally has good prices and good performances.

### Museums
All of the public museums in London have free admission. My favorite is probably the **V&A**, but I also highly recommend the **British Library** if you haven't been — their permanent collection ranges from a Gutenberg bible to original Beatles lyrics written on a napkin in John Lennon's hand. The **National Portrait Gallery** is another favorite.

Some smaller private ones worth checking out (admission likely required): **Leighton House Museum**, **John Soane's Museum**, and **Kenwood House** on Hampstead Heath. If you do go to Kenwood, they have a lovely garden area adjoining their café that's great for an afternoon tea on a nice day.

### Markets

**Borough Market** — Open most days, but Sat and Sun it is definitely in full swing. It's a food market, so arrive hungry and go to nosh. Look for the cider stall to sip while walking around, and also look for the mushroom pâté on offer at one of the stalls. SO delicious. [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Borough+Market+London)

**Brick Lane Market** — Lots of street food, flea market shopping, etc. Sat and Sun. [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Brick+Lane+Market+London)

**Portobello Road Market** — Lots of street food, flea market shopping, etc. Saturdays only. [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Portobello+Road+Market+London)

## Day Trips

### Oxford
Easy to get to by train, and so different in feel and look from London. Go on a walking tour, which grants you access to one or two of the colleges. Highly recommend getting lunch or a drink at a fantastic pub called the **King's Arms**.

### Bath
A little further away, but still doable in a day. Stephanie's mom and her had a great time there. Make sure you go to the Roman Baths — if you're adventurous, you can "take the waters" like all the Georgians.

### Windsor Castle
A quick day trip that can be done in an afternoon. Just hop on a train and you're there in 20-30 min. Castle takes maybe 2-3 hours to "do" and is worth it — great arms and armor collection, and still houses all the Royal "secrets."

### Hampton Court Palace
Main home of Henry VIII. Really neat.

### Kew Gardens
The Royal Botanical Gardens. I haven't been myself, but it's on my list for next time!

## Tips

- Download **CityMapper** — it's like Google Maps but for city transit, and really good about providing all possible public transit routes.
- Consider getting an **eSIM card** for data. 20 GB is super cheap, like $15 USD. You can also get the SIM-card version at most convenience stores.
- If you don't have data, download an **offline map** on Google Maps for London before you go.
- All public museums have free admission — plan accordingly.
- For Borough Market, arrive hungry.
- Portobello Road Market is Saturdays only; Brick Lane is Sat and Sun.

## Eat

#### Deep Dish Pizza

##### Pequod's
Lincoln Park · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Peaquod%27s+Pizza+Chicago)

Their specialty is that they carmelize the crust. Ingredients are fresh and make a difference. About a 20 minute drive from downtown. Deep-dish takes 30-45 min from order — if you like spicy things, get the spicy giardinera as a topping.

##### Art of Pizza
Multiple locations · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Art+of+Pizza+Chicago)

Hole in the wall with great "by the slice" options and no waiting.

##### Lou Malnati's
Multiple locations · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Lou+Malnati%27s+Chicago)

A local chain, but reliably good. Get the sausage — they put a thin layer covering the entire pizza rather than the "meatball" style. Get a butter crust upgrade for added decadence.

##### Gino's East / Pizzeria Uno / Pizzeria Duo
Downtown · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Gino%27s+East+Chicago)

Downtown chains that are all decent entries. I haven't been in a while but hear good things.

*Avoid: Giordano's. Quality isn't great.*

#### Hot Dogs & Italian Beef

**On Chicago-style hotdogs:** Get it with everything — but beware the neon green relish isn't for everyone. Don't put ketchup on it, you may get literally yelled at. In fact, if the restaurant *won't* yell at you for requesting ketchup, it's a bad sign. Get it with fries — the great places wrap the fries up *with* the dog.

**On Italian Beef:** The authentic route is to get it "dipped" in the au-jus. You'll also have the option for sweet or hot peppers, and sometimes cheese.

##### Portillo's
Downtown + multiple locations · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Portillo%27s+Chicago)

Iconic local chain with a downtown location. They also have good Italian Beef. Their chocolate cake is worthwhile. Lots of people like the Chocolate Cake Shake but I think it just clogs up the straw.

##### Al's Beef
Multiple locations · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Al%27s+Beef+Chicago)

Another iconic local chain with locations throughout the city. Simple menu, can't go wrong. The fries are better than Portillo's.

##### The Wiener Circle
Lincoln Park · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Wiener+Circle+Chicago)

A classic unassuming spot by day, an... experience by night after a certain hour, especially on weekends. Search YouTube for examples — be warned, likely NSFW.

#### Donuts

##### The Doughnut Vault
Downtown · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Doughnut+Vault+Chicago)

Tied for 1st place. Small place downtown with incredible simple doughnuts. The chestnut flavor sounds out of left field but is top-notch. Will sell out and close early, so go in the morning.

##### Do-Rite Doughnuts and Chicken
Multiple locations · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Do-Rite+Donuts+Chicago)

Also tied for 1st. A bigger doughnut so you can't have as many. Their chicken is also very good — get both for a well-rounded meal, then find a place for a nap.

##### Firecakes
Downtown · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Firecakes+Donuts+Chicago)

Another good downtown spot, stays open late with good variety.

*Avoid: Stan's. Imported California trash. All showy flavors, terrible quality.*

#### Burgers

##### Au Cheval
Near West Side · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Au+Cheval+Chicago)

Often rated one of the best burgers in the country. Simple burger with an egg on it. No reservations — waits can hit 2+ hours at prime time. Go right when they open or put your name in and grab drinks nearby.

##### Small Cheval
Near West Side · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Small+Cheval+Chicago)

The fast-casual version. Only 3 things on the menu and they're all good. Somewhat pricey for fast-casual, but worth it.

##### Kuma's Corner
Avondale · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Kuma%27s+Corner+Chicago)

A death-metal themed bar with creative, gigantic burgers. Used to be THE best but has dropped a bit since expanding. Still good and a fun experience if you're into the alt scene. Music is loud, campy horror movies play throughout. Don't sleep on the mac and cheese.

##### The Loyalist
West Loop · $$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Loyalist+Chicago)

A fancy restaurant where the burger is all I care about. No idea if the other food is good.

#### Sit-Down Restaurants

##### Le Colonial
Gold Coast · $$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Le+Colonial+Chicago)

French-Vietnamese in the Gold Coast with palm-filled rooms, a breezy terrace, and elegant small plates like chilled shrimp rolls and shaking beef.

##### Ramen-San
River North · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Ramen-San+Chicago)

Quality ramen spot with a fun vibe.

##### Quartino Restaurant
River North · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Quartino+Chicago)

Reasonably priced, unassuming Italian food with unexpectedly fantastic quality.

##### Ema
River North · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Ema+Restaurant+Chicago)

Creative and tasty Mediterranean small plates.

##### Miller's Pub
The Loop · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Miller%27s+Pub+Chicago)

Classic Chicago art-deco dinner spot in the heart of the loop, been around forever. Large menu of traditional American pub fare — nothing fancy, but fun.

##### Green Street Smoked Meats
Fulton Market · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Green+Street+Smoked+Meats+Chicago)

Best BBQ I've had outside of Texas. Order at a counter; the bar inside is a cool hangout spot.

##### Duck Duck Goat
West Loop · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Duck+Duck+Goat+Chicago)

Honestly my favorite Stephanie Izard restaurant. West Loop take on Chinese-American classics — dim sum hits, hand-pulled noodles, bold flavors. Usually easier to get in to than Girl and the Goat. Get the potstickers/dumplings.

##### Wasabi
Logan Square · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Wasabi+Chicago+Logan+Square)

The best ramen I've ever had, in Logan Square. The pork buns and brussel sprouts are killer appetizers.

##### Cho Sun Ok
Lincoln Square · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Cho+Sun+Ok+Chicago)

Incredible Korean BBQ in Lincoln Square. BYOB. Small place — expect a line outside (Winter beware). Always slammed. Don't be put off if the servers aren't buddy-buddy.

##### Virtue
Hyde Park · $$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Virtue+Restaurant+Chicago)

Upscale soul food in Hyde Park. Creative but tasty comfort food — a fun meal, especially for groups who like sharing.

##### Spacca Napoli
Ravenswood · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Spacca+Napoli+Chicago)

Very good Neapolitan-style thin crust pizza in Ravenswood.

##### Flying Saucer
Logan Square · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Flying+Saucer+Chicago)

Creative brunch spot in Logan Square. Vegan/veggie friendly. Specialty is breakfast bowls ranging from healthy-ish to heart-clogging. Expect a 20-40 min wait on weekends.

##### Lula Cafe
Logan Square · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Lula+Cafe+Chicago)

Slightly upscale brunch in Logan Square. The food is always very tasty, the inside is gorgeous. A wait is likely on weekends but will be inside. They'll serve you coffee while you wait.

##### Mi Tocaya Antojería
Logan Square · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Mi+Tocaya+Chicago)

Modern, slightly upscale Mexican in Logan Square. Creative small plates with delicious and unique flavors.

##### Duck Inn
Bridgeport · $$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Duck+Inn+Chicago)

One of our favorite "treat" restaurants. Hidden gem in Bridgeport with a split personality — go full tasting-menu with upscale small plates, or post up at the bar for one of the city's best hot dogs and a solid cocktail.

## Drink

### Three Dots and a Dash
River North · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Three+Dots+and+a+Dash+Chicago)

Chicago-famous tiki bar. Make a reservation or go early (before 5pm) to get a seat at the bar.

### Cindy's Rooftop
The Loop · $$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Cindy%27s+Rooftop+Chicago)

Amazing views and pricey but decent cocktails. Bar is walk-up; food requires a reservation far in advance.

### Chicago Athletic Club
The Loop · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Chicago+Athletic+Association+Hotel)

Same building as Cindy's but slightly less fancy. Cozy vibe with a game room (Bocce, darts, pool) in the back.

### Cherry Circle Room
The Loop · $$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Cherry+Circle+Room+Chicago)

Mad Men-era speakeasy lounge inside the Chicago Athletic Club. Known for their Old Fashioneds. Also serves food.

### Gilt Bar
River North · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Gilt+Bar+Chicago)

Great spot for classic cocktails and upscale French-inspired comfort food. When you check in, ask about The Library — a hidden speakeasy downstairs with velvet booths, vintage books, and the full Gilt menu by candlelight.

### The Aviary
West Loop · $$$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Aviary+Chicago)

A cocktail version of a Michelin 3-star restaurant. Inventive mixes with an emphasis on presentation. Need a reservation. Pricey — individual cocktails are $25-40, or a 3-drink prix-fixe for $75.

### Booze Box / Sushi Dokku
West Loop · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Sushi+Dokku+Chicago)

Speakeasy entrance in an alley, regular street entrance for the restaurant. Very good drinks and very good sushi. Reserve ahead, or bar seats usually open up quickly.

## Do

### The Riverwalk
A beautiful walk along the Chicago River through downtown. Great at any time of year.

### Millennium Park / The Bean
Downtown's main public space. The Bean (Cloud Gate) is iconic for a reason — go early morning for fewer crowds and great reflections.

### The Art Institute of Chicago
World-class museum in Grant Park. The Impressionist and Post-Impressionist collection is one of the best outside of Europe. Reserve tickets in advance.

### Logan Square neighborhood
Wander around Milwaukee Ave for independent shops, coffee, and some of the city's best casual restaurants. Lively weekend farmers market in the square.

### Wicker Park / Bucktown
Another great neighborhood for walking — vintage shops, bars, and restaurants clustered around the six-way intersection at North/Damen/Milwaukee.

## Tips

- Downtown/River North is touristy and you'll pay for it. Head to River West/Fulton Market or Logan Square to escape.
- Deep-dish takes 30-45 min from order — plan accordingly.
- For most doughnut shops, go early for the best selection.
- Au Cheval doesn't take reservations and waits can hit 2+ hours at prime time.
- Cho Sun Ok is BYOB — bring wine or beer.
- The El (elevated train) covers most of the city cheaply; use the CTA app for routes.

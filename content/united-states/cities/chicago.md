## Eat

### Peaquod's
Lincoln Park · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Peaquod%27s+Pizza+Chicago)

Their specialty is that they carmelize the crust. I think their ingredients are also very fresh and make a difference. It's near Lincoln Park, about a 20 minute drive from downtown. Deep-dish takes about 30-45 min to make from time of order — if you like spicy things, get spicy giardinera as a topping.

### Art of Pizza
Multiple locations · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Art+of+Pizza+Chicago)

Hole in the wall joint with several locations that has great "by the slice" options, no waiting here.

### Lou Malnati's
Multiple locations · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Lou+Malnati%27s+Chicago)

A local chain, but reliably good. Get the sausage - they put a thin layer that covers the entire pizza rather than the "meatball" style. Get a butter crust upgrade for added decadence!

### Gino's East / Pizzeria Uno / Pizzeria Duo
Downtown · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Gino%27s+East+Chicago)

Also local chains, but all downtown locations and are decent entries. I haven't been in a while so I can't vouch but hear good things.

*Avoid: Giordano's. Local chain but quality isn't great.*

---

### Portillo's
Downtown + multiple locations · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Portillo%27s+Chicago)

Iconic local chain with a downtown location and you can't go wrong. They also have good Italian Beef. Their chocolate cake is also worthwhile. Lots of people like the Chocolate Cake Shake but I think it just clogs up the straw and is weird when the cake is that mushy.

### Al's Beef
Multiple locations · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Al%27s+Beef+Chicago)

Another iconic local chain with locations throughout the city, simple menu, can't go wrong. The fries are better than Portillo's if I recall.

**On Chicago-style hotdogs:** Get it with everything - but beware the neon green relish isn't for everyone, you won't offend anyone by having things removed. Don't put ketchup on it, you may get literally yelled at. In fact, if the restaurant *won't* yell at you for requesting ketchup on the dog, it's a bad sign. Also get it with fries, the great places wrap the fries up *with* the dog.

**On Italian Beef:** The authentic route is to get it "dipped" in the au-jus. You'll also have the option for sweet or hot peppers, and sometimes cheese. The cheese is going overboard in my opinion, but sometimes that craving hits you.

### The Weiner Circle
Lincoln Park · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Wiener+Circle+Chicago)

A classic unassuming spot by day, an... experience by night after a certain hour, especially on the weekends. Search on Youtube for some examples, be warned, likely NSFW.

---

### The Doughnut Vault
Downtown · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Doughnut+Vault+Chicago)

Tied for 1st place as far as "best". Small place downtown that has incredible simple doughnuts. The chestnut flavor sounds out of left field but is top-notch. More likely to sell out earlier in the day and will close based on whenever they do, so go there early in the morning if possible.

### Do-Rite Doughnuts and Chicken
Multiple locations · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Do-Rite+Donuts+Chicago)

Also tied for 1st place for "best". A bigger doughnut so you can't have as many (up to you...), but still top notch. Their chicken is also very good, they have good lunch sandwiches. Get both for a well-rounded meal, and find a place for a nap after. They are stocked all day, but lose variety the later it gets.

### Firecakes
Downtown · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Firecakes+Donuts+Chicago)

Another good downtown spot, stays open pretty late with good variety.

*Avoid: Stan's. Imported California trash. The glitter of doughnuts - all showy flavors, terrible quality. I literally get sick after having one.*

---

### Au Cheval
Near West Side · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Au+Cheval+Chicago)

Often rated as one of the best burgers in the country. Simple burger with an egg on it with great quality. Be warned - they don't take reservations, and wait times can be 2+ hours at prime time. Get there right when they open (5ish?) or it's downtown adjacent - so a tip is to go put your name in, then grab a cocktail/drinks at a nearby bar that are plentiful near its only location.

### Small Cheval
Near West Side · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Small+Cheval+Chicago)

A fast-casual version of the primetime location. They only serve 3 things on the menu, and they're all good. Somewhat pricey for fast-casual, but worth it!

### Kuma's Corner
Avondale · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Kuma%27s+Corner+Chicago)

A death-metal themed bar with creative, gigantic burgers. It used to be THE best, but since it expanded it has dropped a bit in quality. Still good and noteworthy, and a fun experience, if you're in to the "alt" scene. The music will be loud, and campy horror movies are often playing on TV's throughout the bar. Don't sleep on the mac and cheese.

### The Loyalist
West Loop · $$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Loyalist+Chicago)

A fancy restaurant that serves tons of other pricey stuff, but their burger is all I care about — I have no idea if the other food is good or not...

A mildly unfortunately-named French‑Vietnamese escape in the Gold Coast with fun palm‑filled rooms, a breezy terrace, and a menu of elegant small plates like chilled shrimp rolls and shaking beef.

### Ramen-San
River North · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Ramen-San+Chicago)

Quality ramen spot with a fun vibe.

### Quartino Restaurant
River North · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Quartino+Chicago)

Reasonably priced, unassuming Italian food with unexpectedly fantastic quality.

### Ema
River North · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Ema+Restaurant+Chicago)

Creative and tasty mediterranean small-plates. They had a lunch hot-bar back in the day that was also fantastic, not sure if it is running post-COVID...

### Miller's Pub
The Loop · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Miller%27s+Pub+Chicago)

Classic Chicago art-deco dinner spot in the heart of the loop that has been around forever. Has a large menu with traditional American pub fare, nothing fancy, but fun.

### Green Street Smoked Meats
Fulton Market · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Green+Street+Smoked+Meats+Chicago)

Best BBQ I've had outside of Texas. You order at a counter and the bar inside is a cool hangout spot.

### Duck Duck Goat
West Loop · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Duck+Duck+Goat+Chicago)

Honestly my favorite Stephanie Izard restaurant. It's a West Loop take on Chinese-American classics—dim sum hits, hand-pulled noodles, and bold flavors that are more fun than faithful. Usually the wait isn't too bad and it's much easier to get in to than Girl and the Goat. Get the potstickers/dumplings/whatever!

### Wasabi
Logan Square + River West + Lincoln Square · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Wasabi+Chicago+Logan+Square)

The best Ramen I've ever had, located in Logan Square. They have other similar but different locations in River West and Lincoln Square now. The pork buns and brussel sprouts are also killer appetizer options.

### Cho Sun Ok
Lincoln Square · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Cho+Sun+Ok+Chicago)

Incredible Korean BBQ in Lincoln Square (BYOB). Small place, you may wait in a line for 20-45 min, outside (Winter beware). It's always slammed, and the servers aren't there to coddle you. You're not going to get comedically harassed a la Ed Debevek's/Dick's Last Resort, but don't be put off if the servers aren't buddy-buddy.

### Virtue
Hyde Park · $$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Virtue+Restaurant+Chicago)

Upscale soul food in Hyde Park. All creative but tasty comfort food, and ends up just being a fun meal, especially if your group is in to sharing items.

### Spacca Napoli
Ravenswood · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Spacca+Napoli+Chicago)

Very good neopolitan style thin crust pizza in Ravenswood.

### Flying Saucer
Logan Square / Humboldt Park · $ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Flying+Saucer+Chicago)

A creative brunch spot in Logan Square/Humboldt Park. Vegan/Veggie friendly. They have a creative menu, but also have plenty of breakfast classics. Their specialty is their breakfast bowls which range from healthy-ish to heart clogging. You may have to wait 20-40 min on the weekend and it's small, so that wait may be outside.

### Lula Cafe
Logan Square · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Lula+Cafe+Chicago)

Slightly upscale brunch spot in Logan Square. Stephanie and I have a personal fondness for this place, but can say the food is always very tasty, whether its their creative dishes or the staples. It's bigger and the inside is gorgeous. Again a wait is likely on a weekend, but that wait will be inside if you'd like. A bonus is they'll serve you coffee while you wait (or at least they used to). The area it's in is fun to stroll around if the wait is long.

### Mi Tocaya Antojería
Logan Square · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Mi+Tocaya+Chicago)

Modern, slightly upscale Mexican spot in Logan Square that hits way above taquería-level — creative small plates with delicious and unique flavors.

### Duck Inn
Bridgeport · $$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Duck+Inn+Chicago)

One of our favorite "treat" restaurants. It's a hidden gem in Bridgeport with a split personality — go full tasting-menu with upscale small plates, or post up at the bar for one of the city's best hot dogs and a solid cocktail. The food's always clever but never stuffy.

## Drink

### Three Dots and a Dash
River North · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Three+Dots+and+a+Dash+Chicago)

Chicago-famous tiki bar. Make a reservation ahead of time or go early (earlier than 5pm) to get a seat at the bar.

### Cindy's Rooftop
The Loop · $$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Cindy%27s+Rooftop+Chicago)

Amazing views and pricey but decent cocktails. I've heard the food is really good, but you need a reservation far in advance. Bar is walk-up though.

### Chicago Athletic Club
The Loop · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Chicago+Athletic+Association+Hotel)

Same building as Cindy's but slightly less fancy. Cozy vibe with a fun "game room" (Bacci, darts, pool, etc) area in the back that can be hoppin at happy hours or weekends.

### Cherry Circle Room
The Loop · $$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Cherry+Circle+Room+Chicago)

Madmen-esque era speakeasy lounge in the back of the Chicago Athletic Club. Known for their various Old Fashioned's. A bit pricey but also serves great food. Can get a slightly pricey burger and some apps, or really go for it with $$$ steaks.

### Gilt Bar
River North · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Gilt+Bar+Chicago)

River North spot that nails upscale comfort French-inspired food, but also a great spot for classic cocktails. Bonus - when you check in, ask to see if you can be seated at The Library, a hidden speakeasy downstairs lined with velvet booths, vintage books, and the full Gilt menu served by candlelight.

---

### The Aviary
West Loop · $$$$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Aviary+Chicago)

A cocktail version of the only 3 Michelin Star Restaurant in town. Interesting mixes with an emphasis on presentation. Need a reservation, can probably do 1 week out for non-primetime days, longer for weekends. Pricey, individual cocktails are $25-40ish, or they have a 3-drink prix-fixe for $75.

### Booze Box / Sushi Dokku
West Loop · $$ · [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Sushi+Dokku+Chicago)

Speakeasy entrance in an alley, but the restaurant has a regular street-entrance. Very good drinks and very good sushi. You need a reservation but if you don't mind sitting at the bar something usually opens up quickly.

## Tips

- Downtown/River North is touristy and you'll pay for it — it has some of the less-exciting offerings. Head to River West/Fulton Market to escape "tourist central" if you're pressed for time.
- Deep-dish takes 30-45 min from time of order — plan accordingly.
- For most doughnut shops, get there early to have the best selection.
- Au Cheval doesn't take reservations and waits can hit 2+ hours at prime time — go right when they open or put your name in and grab drinks nearby.
- For Cho Sun Ok Korean BBQ: it's BYOB, expect a line outside (Winter beware), and don't expect the servers to coddle you.
